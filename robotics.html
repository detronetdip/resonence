robo encounter

"It's a licence to kill."

SPACIFICATION

  1. There will be on the dimensions (45cm*45cm*45cm) of the bot.
  2. The weight of the machine should not exceed 15 Kgs. All batteries should be on board. The weight of the remote controller will not be counted.

MOBILITY

    Mobility methods that are NOT allowed:

  1. Flying (using airfoil, helium balloons, ornithopters, etc.) is not allowed.
  2. The robots should not secure itself on the ring surface by using suction cups, diaphragms, sticky treads, glue or other such devices.

ROBOT CONTROL REQUIREMENTS

  1. The robot can be controlled by wired or wireless.
  2. Control must be exhibited over all of its functions and positions. Although autonomous functions within the bot are acceptable, the controller must be able to remotely disable or override these functions at any time. Note that any damage due to this function is the responsibility of the team, and there must compulsorily be a manual emergency stop (E-stop) function that can be controlled from the radio controller to manually override this autonomous function in case of emergency.
  3. If a team has two bots in a same category separate driving system and separate operator should be there.

BATTERY AND POWER

  1. The machine must be powered electrically. Use of an IC engine in any form is not allowed. On-board batteries must be sealed, immobilized-electrolyte types (such as gel cells, lithium, NiCad, NiMH, or dry cells).
  2. The electric voltage between any 2 points on the machine should not exceed 24V DC at any point in time. Participants will have to bring their own converters for standard power supply according to Indian standards.
  3. The participants will be provided standard 220V-60Hz AC supply.

WEAPON SYSTEMS

  1. Robots can have any kind of wedges, Spinner, Disks, lifting devices, hammers etc. (if they qualify the criteria mentioned below) as weapons.
  2. Following weapons cannot be used:
    • Liquid projectiles (Foam, liquefied gases).
    • Any kinds of inflammable liquids.
    • Weapons causing invisible damage (Electrical weapons, RF jamming weapons and others).

MATCH DURATION AND TYPE

Matches will consist of 3 minutes of active fight time exclusive of any time-outs. Hence, it is not binding but advisable to keep battery capacity, power usage and machine defences such that they can sustain a 3 minute fight. The matches can be of the following types:

  1. Match: A regular 1-on-1 combat between two robots
  2. Rumble: A combat between more than two robots simultaneously

A detailed document of rules regarding the format and rules to be followed during the event and the participants will be informed.

CRITERIA FOR VICTORY

  1. Aggression
  2. Control
  3. Damage

* Damaging tracks, college property Or any kind of misbehavior will lead to disqualification.

ROBO RAMP

"Move if you can."

INTRODUCTION

Design a robot either wired or wireless within the specified dimensions that can operate manually and can travel through all obstacles of the track .The smooth and steady Bot will be the winner.

BOT SPECIFICATIONS

  1. The maximum dimension of the Bot can be 25 cm x 25cm x 25 cm (l x b x h). (2% tolerance)
  2. Maximum weight must not exceed 3 kg. (2% tolerance)
  3. The maximum allowable operating voltage should not exceed 12 volts.
  4. The machine must not be made from Lego parts, or any ready-made kit, if we find such a machine it will be disqualified.
  5. The participants will be provided with 220 Volts, 50Hz standard AC supply. Participants will have to arrange themselves for a power adapter.
    6. A bot must not contain any readymade kits, pneumatic & hydraulic systems, IC engines.
  6. Bot must not contain any readymade kits, pneumatic & , hydraulic systems, IC engines.

TEAM RULES

  1. Every TEAM must have a specific name.
  2. Each team should have minimum of 3 members and maximum of 5 members for this event.
  3. All team members must carry a id proof showing their name and college name.

RULES AND REGULATION

  1. A team will be allowed only three times to reset their Bots position during the run. However, this will lead to a penalty.
  2. For the first round if the Bot falls from the arena two times the team will be disqualified. For the final round it will be three times.
  3. Unethical behavior could lead to disqualification.
  4. Judges and co-ordinators have all right to take final decisions for any matter during the event.
  5. Participants will be judged on the basis of points.
  6. No restrictions on wheel sizes, types, or numbers.
  7. Purposefully damaging track or college property will lead to disqualification and Authorities will take proper action.
  8. Only Co-ordinators can touch the bot during the competition time. Touching the bot during the competition by any member of the team will lead to being disqualified.
  9. Rules can be changed in any round by Co-ordinators.

COMPETITION ROUNDS

Round 1

  1. Every team can participate in this round. This round is an elimination round. Teams will be ranked on the basis of their individual score of their performance. Top performers of this round will get into the next round.
Round 2
  1. Co-ordinators will divide teams into groups. Each group contain two teams.
  2. This is a knockout round between the two teams in the same group, one who beats another team of the same group will go to the next round.
  3. The top team from Round-2 makes it to the final round on the basis of total points.

* Damaging tracks, college property Or any kind of misbehavior will lead to disqualification.

robo Striker

"Get set goal."

INTRODUCTION

  1. Build a manual controlled robot designed to take on other robots.
  2. The bot should have proper ground clearance with the arena .
  3. The bot should be capable enough to survive the obstacle given on the arena.
  4. Every time the best Survivor team will be awarded with points.
  5. Teams that are not ready when called for battle will be considered to be declared as walkover & will receive no points.
  6. In all cases ,the co-ordinator team decision will be the final decision.
  7. For the wire bots care should be taken so that the wires do not pull / entangle.

GENERAL RULES

  1. This is a team even.
  2. Each will be a 1 vs. 1 match and the winner progresses to the next round.
  3. Each team will have to defend the goal on its side as well as score the goal on the opposite side.
  4. The minimum participants allowed in a team must be 2 & the maximum number of participants allowed per team is 5.
  5. A team may comprise members from different colleges. No person shall be a member of 2 teams.
  6. Purposefully damaging track or college property will lead to disqualification and Authorities will take proper action.
  7. No bot is allowed to pick the ball up or grip it or incorporate the ball within its body, so that it is not playable for the opponent.
  8. Machine cannot be constructed using ready-made Lego kits or any ready-made mechanism.
  9. The teams must use the same bot which was used in the previous round.
  10. The participants will have to play against each other.
  11. The ball will be placed in the center with robots on both sides of the arena.
  12. 5 minute time will be given to score goals in the opponent’s goal post. The total 5 minute time is divided into two 2.5 minute halves.
  13. In case of a tie. "PENALTY SHOOTOUT" will take place where each team will be given 3 chances.
  14. In the event of any mechanical or electrical failure of a robot, a timeout of maximum 5 minutes can be taken. The timer will be paused during time-out.
  15. If a foul takes place, the bot causing the foul will be taken off the field for 20 seconds.
  16. The winner will be decided by the number of goals scored in minimum time.
  17. Judges and co-ordinators have all right to take final decisions for any matter during the event.
  18. Any or all of the rules are subject to change at any point of time ● A team can be disqualified on disciplinary ground.

TEAM SPECIFICATIONS

  1. A pvc flex sheet of white color with a designed black path is used for the arena. Bots have to follow the black path only.
  2. Every TEAM must have a specific name.
  3. Each team should have a minimum of 3 members and maximum of 5 members for this event.
  4. All team members must carry an id proof showing their name and college name.Team Representation (Leader) at the time of registration.

BOT SPECIFICATION

  1. The size of the bot must not exceed 30 cm*30 cm*30 cm. Individual parameters to be measured with a tolerance of 2%.
  2. The machine must be manually controlled (wire 30 meters).
  3. Maximum weight of each bot should be 5kg.
  4. Touching the robot during the competition is strictly prohibited.
  5. No Lego kits or IC engines are allowed.
  6. Robots must not damage the arena or any other college properties. Else they will be disqualified immediately
  7. The robot may use an external or on board power supply. The potential the difference between any two points on the robot must not exceed 12 V.

* Damaging tracks, college property Or any kind of misbehavior will lead to disqualification.

INTRODUCTION

A manually controlled robots is to be build that can run in water. Get your robot to the Arena consisting of water, and transport the cargo (balls) from the water zone to a cargo/ball deposite box. This task have to be done within a given period of time.

BOT SPECIFICATIONS

  1. The maximum dimension of the bot must not exceed 30 cm x 30 cm x 30cm. (including mechanical arm)
  2. Wired bots must have enough wire length for easy operation and to avoid entanglement with opponent's wires during the course of match. Wires should remain slack during the whole match.
  3. Bot will have only one net holder to pick up the balls from the water arena.

TEAM RULES

  1. Every team must have a specific name and a representative name.
  2. A team may consist of 2-5 members including 1 team leader.

RULES AND REGULATION

  1. The potential difference between any two points should not exceed 12V at any point of time during the match.
  2. An A.C power source of 220 V, 50Hz will be provided at the site of event.
  3. Participating teams are expected to bring their own Eliminators (SMPS) and extension cords as per requirements.
  4. A bot will be declared disqualified, if there is no movement for 30 seconds during the course of the match.
  5. A bot will also be declared disqualified, if any team try to harm the arena.
  6. Negative points will be awarded if you need to touch your boat for any reason during the match.
  7. No negative points shall be awarded for a throw of ball to the outside of the arena.
  8. Organizers will not be responsible for any loss due to power fluctuations.
  9. The Judges will have the final say and the participants will have to abide by the decisions taken by them.

Dark Rider

"Feel the trail."

INTRODUCTION

To build an automated robot which follows a line on its given path.

BOT SPECIFICATIONS

  1. .The robot must fit within a 20cm*20cm*20cm
  2. Bots must be strictly autonomous.
  3. Power supply must be strictly below 12v dc.
  4. Maximum numbers of sensors allowed is 6.
  5. Readymade bots are not allowed.

TRACK SPECIFICATIONS

  1. A pvc flex sheet of white color with a designed black path is used for the arena. Bots have to follow the black path only.
  2. The track will have right and acute angles, junctions, curves and circles.
  3. Width of the black line is 3-5cm.
  4. The track will be placed appropriately with ambient lighting.

MARKING SCHEME

  1. TIME: Marks will be awarded from 100 to 0 serially in an descending order of the rank obtained with a difference of 100 divided by numbers of teams.
  2. OBJECTIVE: Marks will be deduced on the number of times the bot goes of track(2 seconds for each instant) and number of human touches required(5 seconds for each instant)after the timer has been started with a limitation of 5 touches.
  3. INNOVATION AND DESIGN: Maximum of 5 marks for each innovation and designs will be awarded by the jury. The decision of the jury will be irrefutable.
  4. POWER CONSUMPION: Maximum of 2 marks for power consumption will be awarded by the jury. The decision of the jury will be irrefutable.

TEAM RULES

  1. Any team can participate in the line follower event. each team will consist of 1-2members.

RULES AND REGULATION

  1. Minimum participants are 1 and maximum are 2
  2. The robot which will complete the track within least time will be declared as winner.
  3. A bot causing any types of damage to the arena will be disqualified.
  4. The same bot cannot be used by more than one teams and the same team cannot play again with any other name and bot.
  5. Unethical behaviour could lead to disqualification.
  6. In case of a tie the decision of the event heads will be finalized.
  7. Event head and co-ordinators have all right to take final decision for any matter during the event

COMPETITION ROUNDS

Round 1

  1. This is an elimination round and the teams will run their bots on the given track respective to their alloted sequence.
  2. The teams will be ranked in accordance to the score they are awarded by the jury.
  3. Top 4 teams will proceed to the next round.
Round 2
  1. This is a knock out round and the teams will be grouped with 2 teams in each group.
  2. The best of each group will compete between each other.
  3. And the winner will be declared as the winner of the competition.

* Damaging tracks, college property Or any kind of misbehavior will lead to disqualification.

the evaders

"Avoid to win."

INTRODUCTION

Design a automatic ROBOT that has to avoid the obstacles present in the track. One team will compete at a time.

BOT SPECIFICATIONS

  1. The robot must fit within a 20cm x 20cm x 20cm.
  2. Bots must be strictly autonomous.

  4. Maximum number of sensors allowed is 1.

RULES AND REGULATION
